Administrative Support Assistant II, UF Water Institute
⚠ Closes June 25 · Two Days Paid · On-Site · Gainesville, FL

University of Florida Water Institute · Gainesville, FL. The Water Institute at UF is one of the leading interdisciplinary water policy and science research centers in the Southeast, working at the intersection of environmental governance, public health, and resource management. This support role is your foot in the door. The position covers fiscal and administrative support, event coordination for research seminars and workshops, and programmatic tracking for a unit whose work directly touches the policy questions political science students spend four years studying.

But the environment is a research policy institute, and the skills you build here (grant administration, program evaluation, inter-agency coordination) are directly transferable to careers in public administration, environmental policy, and nonprofit program management. Open to students and recent graduates. Strong fit for those interested in environmental governance who want institutional policy experience rather than fieldwork. Apply through the UF HR system by June 25.

Deadline: July 13, 2026 Paid · $65,000–$80,000/yr · SE Region / Remote

Organization Spotlight
National Association for Black Engagement with Asia (NABEA)

If you are interested in U.S.-Asia relations, foreign policy, diplomacy, or international careers and you have not heard of NABEA, this is the introduction you needed. Founded in 2019 by Tyrell Walker, NABEA began when a group of young Black professionals recognized something that has been true for decades: Black Americans are systematically underrepresented in U.S.-Asia engagement, and their perspectives are largely absent from the dialogue that shapes America's most consequential foreign relationships. NABEA was built to change that.

The organization now has more than 400 members working across government, academia, nonprofits, and the private sector in fields touching Asia policy, diplomacy, and international development. Their infrastructure for students and early-career professionals is real and accessible: a Pod Mentor Program connecting members with Black practitioners in the field, a searchable database of Black Asia specialists across disciplines, a robust events calendar, and a growing community of people who are actively navigating space where Black voices have historically been marginalized. Membership is open and the community is one of the most genuinely useful networks in the international affairs space for political science students on an Asia-focused track.

The Mercatus Center at George Mason University's Arlington campus is one of the leading free-market and classical liberal policy research institutions in the country.
