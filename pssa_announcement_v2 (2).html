Organization Spotlight
Black Professionals in International Affairs (BPIA)

If you are interested in international affairs, foreign policy, diplomacy, development, or global public service, BPIA should be on your radar immediately. Founded in 1989, BPIA exists specifically to increase the involvement of African Americans and people of African descent in international affairs. They are not just a networking organization; they have infrastructure for students: a mentorship program, an opportunity board, an expert database, a resume job bank, collegiate chapters, an annual career fair, and a rich calendar of programming tied to major convenings like UNGA, the IMF/World Bank Annual Meetings, and the World Economic Forum.

The mentorship program in particular is worth your attention, it connects students and emerging professionals directly with Black practitioners working across the field. This is the kind of sustained relationship-building that opens doors that are otherwise hard to find on your own.

Freeman-ASIA Study Abroad Scholarship
Deadline: April 7, 2026 Up to $7,000 · Undergrad · Need-Based

U.S. Department of State / IIE. Freeman-ASIA provides need-based scholarships for U.S. undergraduates with demonstrated financial need who are planning to study abroad in East or Southeast Asia (Cambodia, China, Indonesia, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, Thailand, Vietnam, and more). Awards range from $3,000 (summer) to $7,000 (full year). U.S. citizens and permanent residents eligible. You must work with your study abroad advisor to complete the application, plan accordingly and do not wait until the last week.
