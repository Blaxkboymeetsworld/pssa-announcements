Scientists in Parks, Winter 2026-27 Positions, National Park Service
⚠ Closes June 14, 2026 Paid · AmeriCorps · Full-Time · Nationwide

National Park Service / Conservation Legacy. Scientists in Parks places students and early-career professionals in full-time, paid AmeriCorps positions at national parks across the country. Positions run from 3 months to one year and span natural resource fields including biological sciences, physical sciences, social sciences, and science communications. For students in public policy or environmental policy, this is a direct federal pathway into public lands work with hands-on experience inside the agency. The application closes June 14 at 11:59 PM EDT, or earlier if a position hits 60 applicants. Apply early and apply to multiple positions if you qualify for them.

Legacy Places Intern, The Conservation Fund
⚠ Closes June 14, 2026 Paid · $23/hr · Remote (Georgia)

The Conservation Fund, Remote. The Underground Railroad is one of the most significant chapters in American history, and a meaningful number of its sites remain unprotected, underfunded, and at risk of being lost permanently. The Conservation Fund's Legacy Places program exists to prevent that. This internship puts you directly inside that mission: you will research and map unprotected sites along the Underground Railroad and freedom corridors, help visualize where preservation is most urgent, and build a network of government and nonprofit partners positioned to act. The total engagement is 375 hours with fully negotiable start and end dates. Through this work you will develop skills in archival research, data visualization, mapping, and historic preservation that are directly applicable to careers in public policy, urban planning, and civil society. At $23/hr remote, this is among the most substantive and distinctive opportunities in this drop.

Fall 2026 Internship, Federalist Society
Closes July 1, 2026 Unpaid · Academic Credit · Washington, D.C.

The Federalist Society for Law and Public Policy Studies, Washington, D.C. Fall applications close July 1. Interns work on substantive research, editing, and programming across the National Lawyers Convention, Practice Groups, State Courts and Article I Projects, and Student Division. Administrative work is minimal. Open to undergraduates, graduate students, and law students. This is an unpaid position; academic credit can be arranged. Fellowship Spotlight
National Defense Fellowship, Alexander Hamilton Society & Ronald Reagan Institute

The National Defense Fellowship is a joint program between the Alexander Hamilton Society and the Ronald Reagan Institute that selects 20 advanced undergraduates and graduate students annually for an immersive experience in U.S. national security and defense policy. Travel, accommodations, and meals throughout both components are fully covered.

The first component is the Peace Through Strength Boot Camp, a four-day intensive in Washington, D.C. from September 24-27. Fellows learn directly from senior defense policy practitioners on strategy, civil-military relations, force structure, the industrial base, and the future of warfare. The second is attendance at the Reagan National Defense Forum on December 4-5 at the Reagan Presidential Library in Simi Valley, California, one of the premier annual gatherings of defense professionals in the country, with speakers including Secretaries of Defense, Directors of CIA, and Chairmen of the Joint Chiefs of Staff.

Applications for the 2026 cohort are open now. Twenty spots for a national program is a small cohort. Do not sit on this one, apply sooner rather than later. Questions can go to timothy@hamsoc.org.

Apply at alexanderhamiltonsociety.org →
Pre-Law Track
Litigation Fellow (Fall 2026), Institute for Justice
Actively Recruiting Paid · 2 Years · Multiple Offices

For recent law graduates or post-clerkship candidates with under two years of experience. The Institute for Justice is the nation's leading libertarian public interest law firm, litigating constitutional cases on economic liberty, free speech, civil forfeiture, and property rights. Their Litigation Fellowship is a two-year appointment starting August/September 2026 at offices in Arlington (HQ), Austin, Miami, Phoenix, and Seattle. Fellows litigate real cases alongside senior attorneys from day one, with expert media training and a mentorship structure designed to develop constitutional litigators. Clerkship experience is preferred but not required, and IJ offers tiered bonuses for candidates coming directly from federal or state supreme court clerkships.

Know This Network: Federalist Society Student Division
$5/Year Student Membership Chapters at Every Law School

For pre-law and law students at every stage. The Federalist Society is the dominant conservative legal network in the country, with chapters at virtually every accredited law school. Alumni include sitting Supreme Court justices, federal appellate judges, senators, and cabinet officials. Student membership is $5/year and gives access to chapter events with practitioners and judges, national convenings, and the annual Student Symposium. You do not have to be conservative to benefit from knowing this organization. If public law, constitutional litigation, or government service is on your radar, this is a professional relationship worth building early.
